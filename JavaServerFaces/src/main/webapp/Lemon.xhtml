Lemon - a small evergreen fruit tree up to 5-8 m, with spreading or pyramidal crown. From prevention to treatment and lemon drink with hypovitaminosis, beriberi, diseases of the gastrointestinal tract, the disturbance of mineral metabolism, rheumatism, kidney stones, atherosclerosis, scurvy, sore throat, gout, high blood pressure. In the Middle Ages believed that the lemon prevents the plague, and is an antidote for snake bites. Eastern medicine is considered an excellent means of a lemon to treat wounds and pulmonary diseases and an antidote for various poisonings. In the XI century. Avicenna wrote about the lemon as the best medicine for heart disease, recommended eating it, and pregnant women with jaundice. Currently, lemon juice and lemon oil obtained from fresh skin, used to improve taste and odor of drugs. There have been attempts to use lemon juice for the treatment of edema and urine acid diathesis, tincture of lemon peel or zest - as a tool that increases the appetite, sedative and antiemetic. In folk medicine, the lemon was used as a remedy for scurvy vitamin, to lubricate the throat diphtheritic plaque, as an additional remedy for jaundice and liver disease, edema, kidney stones, rheumatism, gout, gastritis with low acidity, lemon syrup - as antihelmintic; External solution in water, juice rinse your mouth with sore throat and inflammation of the mouth, used for lotions for fungal infections of the skin and eczema. Lemon is widely used as a cosmetic - lemon water softens and whitens the skin, it is used in a mixture with the beaten egg white, glycerin and cologne to get rid of freckles, age spots, rejuvenate skin. Lemon juice heals cracks in the skin, reduces the brittleness of nails. Lemon rind, boiled in honey, used to improve digestion. In cosmetic purposes Lemon balm is used as hair creams, lotions, washes, and for the manufacture of masks for the care of a variety of skin types.
